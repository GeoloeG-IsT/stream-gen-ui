# Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

### How do I contact the city?
For general information and non-emergency inquiries, call the City Information Line at +49 30 2300 0311 (Monday-Friday 7:00-19:00, Saturday 9:00-13:00). For specific departments, see our contact directory at berlin-city.de/contacts. For emergencies, always call 112.

---

### Where is City Hall located?
City Hall is located at Hauptstraße 100, Berlin. The building is open Monday-Friday 8:30-16:30. Public parking is available in the garage on Nebenstraße (2-hour validation available). The building is accessible by bus lines 12, 45, 67 and tram lines 1, 3. Bike racks are available at the main entrance.

---

### How do I pay my property taxes?
Property taxes can be paid online at berlin-city.de/payments, by mail (check payable to "City of Berlin"), or in person at the Finance Building (Finanzstraße 34). Payments are due quarterly on January 31, April 30, July 31, and October 31. You can also set up automatic payments through the online portal.

**Contact:** Wolfgang Kaiser (+49 30 2306 9100)

---

### How do I register for recreation programs?
Register online at berlin-city.de/recreation or in person at the Community Center (Monday-Friday 9:00-17:00, Saturday 9:00-13:00). Some programs fill quickly, so register early! Program catalogs are available online and at the Community Center, libraries, and district offices.

**Contact:** Monika Fischer (+49 30 2308 2110)

---

### Where can I find city job openings?
Current job openings are posted at berlin-city.de/jobs. Applications must be submitted online through the employment portal. For questions about the application process, contact Human Resources at hr@berlin-city.de or +49 30 2300 0500.

## Services Questions

### How do I get a building permit?

1. **Pre-application consultation:** Schedule a meeting with the Building Permits office to discuss your project (+49 30 2305 8120)
2. **Prepare plans:** Have an architect or designer prepare detailed construction plans
3. **Submit application:** Submit plans and application online or in person
4. **Plan review:** Staff reviews plans for code compliance (typically 2-4 weeks)
5. **Approval and payment:** Once approved, pay permit fees
6. **Construction:** Begin work after receiving permit
7. **Inspections:** Schedule required inspections during construction
8. **Certificate of occupancy:** Obtain final approval before occupying building

**Timeline:** Simple projects (decks, additions): 2-4 weeks. Complex projects (new construction): 6-12 weeks.

**Contact:** Kristina Mayer (kristina.mayer@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2305 8120)

---

### How do I report a pothole?
Report potholes online at berlin-city.de/report or call the City Information Line at +49 30 2300 0311. Provide the exact location (address or cross streets). You'll receive a confirmation number to track the repair. Emergency road hazards are addressed within 24 hours; routine potholes within 5-7 business days.

**Contact:** Robert Zimmerman (+49 30 2303 6120)

---

### How do I start a business in Berlin?

1. **Research requirements:** Check zoning to ensure your business type is allowed at your location
2. **Register your business:** Register with the tax office for tax ID number
3. **Apply for business license:** Submit application to Finance Department with required documentation
4. **Obtain additional permits:** Some businesses need health permits, alcohol licenses, or special permits
5. **Business tax registration:** Register for business tax with Finance Department
6. **Open for business:** Once all licenses and permits are approved

**Resources:** Attend a free Small Business Development Workshop (offered quarterly) or schedule a consultation with Economic Development (+49 30 2306 9130).

**Contact:** Martin Schroeder (martin.schroeder@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2306 9110)

---

### How do I apply for affordable housing assistance?
Contact the Housing Assistance Programs office to schedule an intake appointment (+49 30 2310 4105). Bring proof of income, identification, and current housing information. Staff will determine eligibility and explain available programs including rental vouchers and assistance programs.

**Walk-in applications:** Tuesday, Thursday 9:00-11:00 (no appointment needed)

**Contact:** Peter Neumann (peter.neumann@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2310 4105)

---

### How do I request public records?
Submit requests online at berlin-city.de/records or contact the Public Records Office (+49 30 2307 1110). Provide a detailed description of the records you need. Most requests are processed within 5 business days. Some records may have associated copying costs.

**Contact:** Lars Schmidt (lars.schmidt@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2307 1110)

---

### How do I get a parking permit?
Residential parking permits can be obtained online at berlin-city.de/parking or in person at the East District Office or Transportation Center. You'll need proof of residency and vehicle registration. Annual permits cost €50. Monthly guest permits also available.

**Contact:** Hans Krueger (hans.krueger@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2303 6130)

---

### How do I apply for a street tree?
Submit requests for street tree planting through the online portal at berlin-city.de/trees or contact the Urban Forestry office. Trees are planted based on available locations, species appropriateness, and funding. Planting typically occurs in spring or fall. There is no cost to property owners.

**Contact:** Felix Becker (felix.becker@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2308 2105)

---

### How do I dispose of large items?
Schedule bulk item pickup online at berlin-city.de/waste or call +49 30 2302 5130. Pickup is available twice per year per household at no additional charge. Items include furniture, appliances, mattresses (no electronics or hazardous materials). Set items curbside the evening before your scheduled pickup day.

**Contact:** Stefan Richter (stefan.richter@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2302 5130)

## Meeting and Government Questions

### When does the City Council meet?
The City Council meets on the first Wednesday of each month at 19:00 in the City Hall Council Chamber. Public comment begins at 19:00 (sign up starts at 18:30). Full agendas are posted 48 hours before meetings at berlin-city.de/council. Meetings are also livestreamed.

---

### How do I speak at a City Council meeting?
Sign up for public comment starting at 18:30 on meeting night at the Council Chamber. You may also submit written comments in advance to clerk@berlin-city.de. Each speaker is allotted 3 minutes. Topics should relate to city business or agenda items.

---

### How do I find City Council agendas and minutes?
Current and past agendas, minutes, and meeting videos are available at berlin-city.de/council. Documents are typically posted 48 hours before meetings. Approved minutes are posted within two weeks after meetings.

---

### How do I watch City Council meetings?
Attend in person at City Hall Council Chamber, watch the livestream at berlin-city.de/live, or view archived meetings on the city website or YouTube channel (youtube.com/BerlinCityGov).

---

### What boards and commissions can I join?
Berlin has numerous boards and commissions including Planning Commission, Historic Preservation Commission, Parks Board, and Transportation Advisory Committee. View current openings and application information at berlin-city.de/boards. Applications are reviewed as vacancies occur.

---

## Utilities Questions

### How do I set up utility service?
For new service, call Customer Service at +49 30 2302 5100 or apply online at berlin-city.de/utilities. You'll need your move-in date, property address, and identification. Service can typically be established within 2 business days.

**Contact:** Klaus Weber (klaus.weber@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2302 5100)

---

### What do I do if my power goes out?
First, check if neighbors have power (may be isolated to your property). Check your circuit breaker. If the outage is widespread, report it to the emergency line at +49 30 2302 5000. Check berlin-city.de/outages for outage maps and restoration estimates.

**Emergency Contact:** +49 30 2302 5000 (24/7)

---

### How is my water bill calculated?
Water bills are based on metered usage plus fixed service charges. Bills are issued quarterly. Rates are available at berlin-city.de/utilities. If you believe your bill is incorrect, contact Customer Service at +49 30 2302 5100 for a meter reading verification.

---

### What items can I recycle?
Berlin accepts paper, cardboard, glass, metal cans, and plastic containers #1-7 in curbside recycling bins. Flatten cardboard, rinse containers, and keep items loose (no bags). For complete recycling guidelines, visit berlin-city.de/recycling or contact Emma Fischer (+49 30 2302 5135).

---

### When is trash collection in my neighborhood?
View your collection schedule online at berlin-city.de/waste by entering your address. Collections occur weekly (trash) and biweekly (recycling). Place bins curbside by 7:00 AM on collection day. Report missed collections within 24 hours to +49 30 2302 5130.

---

## Transportation Questions

### How do I report a traffic signal problem?
Report malfunctioning traffic signals to the emergency line at +49 30 2303 6000 (24/7). For non-emergency signal timing concerns, contact Sophie Wagner at +49 30 2303 6135 during business hours.

---

### How do I request a traffic study?
Submit traffic study requests to the Transportation Department (+49 30 2303 6135). Include the location, specific concerns (speeding, accidents, visibility), and any supporting information. Staff will assess the request and schedule a study if warranted.

**Contact:** Sophie Wagner (sophie.wagner@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2303 6135)

---

### How do I request a bus stop or route change?
Contact the Transit Services Coordinator (+49 30 2303 6100) with your request. Provide specific details about the proposed change and community need. Requests are evaluated based on ridership potential, service equity, and operational feasibility.

**Contact:** Petra Schneider (petra.schneider@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2303 6100)

---

### How do I reserve sports fields or facilities?
Reserve facilities online at berlin-city.de/facilities or in person at the Community Center or Sports Complex. Reservations accepted up to 60 days in advance. Fees vary by facility and user type (residents/non-residents). Seasonal permits available for sports leagues.

**Contact:** Stefan Meyer (stefan.meyer@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2308 2115)

## Health and Safety Questions

### Where can I get vaccinated?
Immunization clinics are held at the Health Services Building (Gesundheitsweg 22). Walk-in hours: Tuesday, Thursday 13:00-16:00; Wednesday 9:00-12:00. Appointments available Monday-Friday 9:00-17:00. Call +49 30 2304 7125 for information.

**Contact:** Maria Schulz (maria.schulz@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2304 7125)

---

### How do I report a health code violation?
Report food safety concerns, unsanitary conditions, or other health code violations to Environmental Health at +49 30 2304 7120. All complaints are investigated. You may report anonymously if preferred.

**Contact:** Frank Becker (frank.becker@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2304 7120)

---

### Where can I get help in a mental health crisis?
Call the Mental Health Crisis Line at +49 30 2304 7200 (24/7). Trained counselors provide immediate support and can dispatch mobile crisis teams if needed. For non-emergency mental health services, contact Laura Stein at +49 30 2304 7110.

---

### How do I prepare for emergencies?
Start with an emergency kit including water, non-perishable food, flashlight, radio, first aid supplies, and medications for at least 3 days. Sign up for emergency alerts at berlin-city.de/alerts. Consider taking free CERT training (+49 30 2301 4135). Download the Emergency Preparedness Guide at berlin-city.de/prepare.

**Contact:** David Kowalski (david.kowalski@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2301 4130)

---

### How do I report housing code violations?

**Contact:** Robert Fischer (robert.fischer@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2310 4115)

## Library and Cultural Questions

### How do I get a library card?
Visit any library location with proof of address (utility bill, driver's license). Cards are free for residents and good for three years. Use your card to check out books, access digital resources, reserve computers, and register for programs.

**Contact:** Central Library (+49 30 2309 3100)

---

### How do I reserve a meeting room?
Meeting rooms are available at City Hall, libraries, and the Community Center. Reserve online at berlin-city.de/rooms or contact the specific facility. Some locations charge fees for non-city groups. Nonprofit organizations may receive discounted rates.

---

### How do I donate historical items to the museum?
Contact the Museum Director to discuss potential donations (+49 30 2309 3110). The museum collects items related to Berlin's history including photographs, documents, artifacts, and memorabilia. Not all donations can be accepted due to space and relevance considerations.

**Contact:** Dr. Elisabeth Wagner (elisabeth.wagner@berlin-city.de, +49 30 2309 3110)
